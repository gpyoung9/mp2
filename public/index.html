Movies

Click the posters to get a summary and my thoughts on the movie!

Princess Mononoke

"On a journey to find the cure for a Tatarigami's curse, Ashitaka finds himself in the middle of a war between the forest gods and Tatara, a mining colony. In this quest he also meets San, the Mononoke Hime."

This is a movie that my dad showed me and my brother when we were kids that I did not really appreciate at the time. As I grew older and watched it again I began to understand its themes and motivations and it quickly found its place in my heart.

Howl's Moving Castle

"When an unconfident young woman is cursed with an old body by a spiteful witch, her only chance of breaking the spell lies with a self-indulgent yet insecure young wizard and his companions in his legged, walking castle."

This is also a movie that my dad showed me as a kid. I've always loved this steampunk/fantasy type setting that exists in this movie as well as the atmosphere that it creates. The soundtrack used in this film is also something that has been dear to me for a very long time and does a great job of enhancing the environment.

Lord of the Rings

"A meek Hobbit and eight companions set out on a journey to destroy the One Ring and the Dark Lord Sauron."

Every family has their movie series that they connect with. For some it's Star Wars, for others it's Harry Potter. My family has always been a fan of fantasy adventure so naturally we gravitated towards the Lord of the Rings trilogy. I've watched this trilogy well over 20 times in my life and still find as much enjoyment from it as I did as a kid. I remember always thinking that Legolas was the coolest character in the entire series because of his archery and natural suave. These movies are ones that I have the best memories of.
